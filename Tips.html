    Packing

    1. 1. To maximize space in your luggage, Roll your clothes, don’t fold.

    2. 2. Stay organize and create a list to ensure you bring what will be needed.

    3. 3. Pack smartly. Bring garments that will serve serval purposes.

    4. 4. When Packing toiletries, I recommend you place them in zip-lock bags to avoid spills.

    5. 5. Pack your essentials in your carry-on. Think of your daily routine, and don’t forget your travel documents.

    Flights

    1. 1. Investigate your airline's baggage-fee policy.

    2. 2. Layer. Put on several garments to overweight avoid fees.

    3. 3. Be aware of the security rules, avoid items that will be removed like: large shampoo, water bottle, etc...

    4. 4. Remember most airline are quite harsh, when it comes to transporting our luggage. Make sure your luggage is sturdy enough to withstand the impacts and take any branded luggage with you as carry on, to avoid a bad experience.

    Saftey

    1. 1. Do your research on your destinations and crime activity.

    2. 2. Do not flash. Put away your jewels and watches when walking streets, you are unfamiliar with.

    3. 3. Do not be too trusting when using public wi-fi. Leave all bank activity or important information for a safe network.

    4. 4. Avoid distraction like headphone or such. Always be award of your surroundings.

    Transportation

    1. 1. Many hotels will provide you with their private transportation to and from airports, always ask.

    2. 2. Taxis: Make sure they are a certified Cab company. these companies will have permission to be at ports an airport non-certified will be banned.

    3. 3. Personal driver: Avoid using uber or such in 3rd world countries. Instead hire services your hotel recommends.

    4. 4. Trust your hotel they always and I mean always have number to taxis services they trust and know.

    Hotels

    1. 1. Wipe down you remote, most hotel do not sanitize their remote control after every stay.

    2. 2. Do not place your luggage on the beds. Not all but some hotel will have bed and you are basically giving them a free ride to your home on your luggage.

    3. 3. Check for outlets. As you exit US and enter Europe, the power outlet changes, make sure you have the necessary outlets to charge your devices.

    4. 4. Lock it up. Place a lock on your luggage when you leave your room.

    5. 5. Hotel laundry service can be a big increase on you bill. Check for laundry services around most hotel corners where these small businesses thrive.

    6. 6. Last, but certainly not least. DO NOT FORGET TO CHECK YOUR CHECK-IN TIME. If this time frame is long from your arrival time, call your hotel. Most hotel's will accommodate on a late check-in. If it’s an early check-in, they can still accommodate (If the room is ready), but most time for a small fee.
